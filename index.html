About APJ Abdul Kalam

APJ Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and passed away on 27 July 2015. He is widely regarded as the "People's President" for his down-to-earth personality and his efforts to reach out to the common man.

Achievements

APJ Abdul Kalam was a distinguished scientist and engineer who made significant contributions to India's civilian space program and military missile development. He played a pivotal role in the development of the country's first satellite launch vehicle and the Agni missile. He was awarded the Padma Bhushan in 1981, the Padma Vibhushan in 1990, and the Bharat Ratna in 1997.

Speeches

APJ Abdul Kalam was a great orator and his speeches inspired millions of people. Some of his famous speeches include:"Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action. I had the privilege to speak in front of the European Parliament on the importance of science and technology in today's world. My message was simple - science is the bridge between the past and the future, and we must invest in it to ensure a better tomorrow."
